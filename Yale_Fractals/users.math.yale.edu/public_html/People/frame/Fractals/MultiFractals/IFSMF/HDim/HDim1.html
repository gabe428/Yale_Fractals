Hausdorff dimension bounded by measure

If the measure of discs about every point of a set A has a power law scaling with exponent s, then the s-dimensional Hausdorff measure is bounded by the measure of A.
Specifically, let De(x) denote the disc of radius e centered at x and suppose
    lime -> 0mu(Be(x))/es <= c for all x in A.
    then Hs(A) >= mu(A)/c.
