Tau(q) exercise solutions

Pictured here are two graphs of some points on the tau(q) graph for these values: r = 0.5, p1 = 0.05, p2 = 0.2, p3 = 0.3, and p4 = 0.45.
   
Examining these pictures we see visual evidence that this tau(q) curve has oblique asymptotes.
To estimate the slope of the q -> -infinity asymptote, compute the slope determined by the points (-4, tau(-4)) = (-4, 17.29) and (0, 0), obtaining 17.29/(-4) = -4.32.
To estimate the slope of the q -> infinity asymptote, compute the slope determined by the points (12, tau(12)) = (12, -13.81) and (0, 0), obtaining (-13.81)/12 = -1.15.
We have seen the slope of the q -> infinity asymptote is -max(log(pi)log(ri)), and the slope of the q -> infinity asymptote is -min(Log(pi)Log(ri)).
For these values - all ri = 0.5 and pi = 0.05, 0.2, 0.3, and 0.45 - we have
Log(pi)/Log(ri) = 4.32, 2.32, 1.74, 1.15
Our estimates appear to have been quite good.
