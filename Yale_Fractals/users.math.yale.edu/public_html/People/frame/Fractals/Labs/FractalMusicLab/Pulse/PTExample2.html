Pulse Trains

What if we want to listen to more than one pulse train at a time?
If we were to play the percussive tracks from the previous examples simultaneously, we would only hear the highest frequency click; coincident pulses would all sound the same.
One solution might be to adjust the volume of the clicks in proportion to their depth.
This can work, but only to a limited extent; rapid, subtle changes in volume are difficult for the ear to discern clearly.

A better approach is to use different voices to represent different depths.
Here are the voice assignments for this orchestrated rendition of the same piece:

Generally speaking, lower frequency pulse trains dominate all coincident pulses belonging to higher frequency pulse trains.
Consider, for example, the way the thump of a bass drum or slap of a bass guitar can momentarily overshadow other voices.
