6. (mod 9) Shading the boxes of Pascal's triangle with numbers congruent to

0 (mod 9) 1, ... 7, or 8 (mod 9) 1, 2, 4, 5, 7, or 8 (mod 9)

Observe Z9 has one proper subgroup,
    H = {0,3,6}, ispmorphic to Z3.
Then Z9/H is isomorphic to Z3 and the union of cosets 1 + H and 2 + H, corresponds to {1,2} in Z3. This explains the third picture.
