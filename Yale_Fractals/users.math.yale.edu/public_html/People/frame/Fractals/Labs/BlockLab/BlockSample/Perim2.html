The second level consists of three copies of the first level, each scaled by a factor of 1/2.
The perimeter of the first level is made up of 22 edges, each of length 1, so our first guess might be that the perimeter of the second level is made up of 3*22 edges, each of length 1/2.
Notice the three green edges belong to the perimeter of both the lower left and lower right scaled copies of the first stage, yet do not belong to the perimeter of the second stage. Together account for 6 of the 3*22 edges not contributing to the perimeter.
The three blue edges belong to the perimeter of both the lower right and upper scaled copies of the first stage, yet do not belong to the perimeter of the second stage. Together account for 6 of the 3*22 edges not contributing to the perimeter.
The red edge does contribute to the perimeter. It is an edge of the lower left and upper scaled copies of the first stage, so accounts for 1 of the 3*22 edges not contributing to the perimeter.
(The orange and purple edges do contribute to the perimeter. The orange edge belongs to the perimeter of the lower right, but not the lower left, scaled copy of the first stage. Including it does not alter those of the 3*22 edges contributing to the perimeter. The purple edge is analogous.)
So the total perimeter is
P2 = 3*22*(1/2) - 6*(1/2) - 6*(1/2) - 1*(1/2) = (3*22 - 13)*(1/2)
