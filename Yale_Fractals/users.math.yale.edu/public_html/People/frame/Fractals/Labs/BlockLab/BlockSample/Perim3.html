The third level consists of three copies of the second level, each scaled by a factor of 1/2.
The perimeter of the second level is made up of 3*22 - 13 edges, each of length 1/2, so our first guess might be that the perimeter of the third level is made up of 3*(3*22 - 13) edges, each of length 1/4.
Notice the three green edges belong to the perimeter of both the lower left and lower right scaled copies of the second stage, yet do not belong to the perimeter of the third stage. Together account for 6 of the 3*(3*22 - 13) edges not contributing to the perimeter.
The three blue edges belong to the perimeter of both the lower right and upper scaled copies of the second stage, yet do not belong to the perimeter of the third stage. Together account for 6 of the 3*(3*22 - 13) edges not contributing to the perimeter.
The red edge does contribute to the perimeter. It is an edge of the lower left and upper scaled copies of the second stage, so accounts for 1 of the 3*(3*22 - 13) edges not contributing to the perimeter.
So the total perimeter is
    P3 = 3*(3*22 - 13)*(1/4) - 6*(1/4) - 6*(1/4) - 1*(1/4)
    = (3*(3*22 - 13) - 13)*(1/4) = (32*22 - 13*(1 + 3))*(1/4)
