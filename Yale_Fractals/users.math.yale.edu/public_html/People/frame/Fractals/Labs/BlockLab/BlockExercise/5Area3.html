The third level consists of 5 scaled copies of the second level, but to see the pattern more clearly, it is easier to view the third level as consisting of 52= 25 scaled copies of the first stage.
The bases of 16 of these copies reconstitute the base of the first level.
The bases of 4 of these copies reconstitute the new base of the second level.
The remaining 5 copies contribute 5 new bases, squares of area = (1/4)2.
Arguing as before about the overlaps becoming points in the limiting shape, we see these bases indeed are part of the area of the shape.
Combining levels 1, 2, and 3, we see A3 = 1 + 1/4 + 5*(1/4)2 = 25/16.
Whatever it is, the area of the limiting shape is at least 25/16.
