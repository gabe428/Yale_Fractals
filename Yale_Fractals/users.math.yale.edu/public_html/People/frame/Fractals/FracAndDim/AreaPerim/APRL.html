For fractal curves of dimension d, the number N(a) of segments of length a needed to traverse the curve scales as
N(a) = k⋅(1/a)d
for some constant k. Consequently, the length P(a) measrured at scale a is
P(a) = N(a)⋅a = k⋅a1-d
If the length is measured at another scale b, then
P(b) = N(b)⋅b = k⋅b1-d
Comparing measurements yields
P(a)/P(b) = (a/b)1-d
