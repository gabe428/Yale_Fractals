Large-Scale Distribution of Galaxies

Coleman applies a conditional correlation analysis and deduces a fractal structure over a much larger scale.
Current evidence suggests a fractal distribution of galaxies out to 600 million light years, and there is yet no compelling evidence to believe the fractal distribution of galaxies does not extend throughout the universe.
Pietronero and coworkers have deduced a dimension of 2 for the galaxy distribution up to 600 million light years.
