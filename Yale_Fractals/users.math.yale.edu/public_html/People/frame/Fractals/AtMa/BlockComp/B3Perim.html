Block Fractals

The dimensions of the cubes are divided by 1/2 at each successive generation, so the thickness of the limiting shape is 0.
In fact, the limiting shape is a right isosceles Sierpinski gasket.
So it suffices to compute the perimeter of the gasket.
    (left) The outer boundary, B0, of the gasket has length 2 + sqrt(2).
    (center) The largest inner boundary, B1, has length (2 + sqrt(2))/2.
    (right) The next largest inner boundaries, B2, have lengths summing to 3(2 + sqrt(2))/4.
In general, Bn has lengths summing to 3n-1(2 + sqrt(2))/2n = (3/2)n-1(2 + sqrt(2))/2.
So Bn -> infinity as n -> infinity, and consequently the gasket has infinite perimeter.
