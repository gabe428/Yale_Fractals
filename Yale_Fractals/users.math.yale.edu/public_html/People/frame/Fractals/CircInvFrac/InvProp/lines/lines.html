Circle Inversion Fractals

Properties of Inversion

Suppose the circle B passes through the center of the inverting circle A.
The center of A inverts in A to the point at infinity, so B inverts to a straight line.
We distinguish three cases
    B intersects A in two points.
    B intersects A in one point.
    B intersects A in not at all.

This can be made rigorous by taking appropriate limits of the formulas for the inverse of a circle.
