Addresses in the Koch curve

Every point of the Koch curve has some address, an infinite sequence of 0s, 1s, 2s, and 3s.
The two cases investigated already deal with sequences that terminate in a constant infinite string.
Given any address a1a2a3... that does not terminate in a constant string, chords from a1a2a3...ak(0infinity) to a1a2a3... will not make a constant angle with the horizontal because some of the additional ai will be different from ai-1.
