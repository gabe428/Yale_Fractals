Addresses in the Koch curve

Using the standard decomposition ofthe Koch curve into four parts, we can assign addresses 0, 1, 2, and 3 to those parts.
Parts of parts have corresponding addresses.
Points where parts join have multiple addresses. For example, the right-most point of part 0 has address 0333... and the left-most point of part 1 has address 1000.... In the Koch curve these points are identical.
As an application of this addressing notion, we show that at no point of the Koch curve can a tangent be defined.
    First, consider points with non-unique addresses.
    Next, we consider points with addresses ending in an infinite sequence of 1s or an infinite sequence of 2s.
    From these examples the general approach now should be clear.
