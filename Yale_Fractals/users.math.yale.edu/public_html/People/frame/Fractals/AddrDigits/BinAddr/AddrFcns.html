Addresses and Functions

The scalings we visualized were these:
    shrink the inch to the left half-inch, and
    shrink the inch to the right half-inch,
Placing the origin at the left edge of the inch, these scalings can be written as the results of these functions
    f0(x) = x/2, and
    f1(x) = x/2 + 1/2.
Calling I0, I1, I00, I01, ... the regions with addresses 0, 1, 00, 01, ..., we see
I0 = f0([0,1])  I1 = f1([0,1])
I00 = f0(f0([0,1]))  I01 = f0(f1([0,1]))
That is, address 01 lies in the left (0) half, within which it is the right (1) half.
