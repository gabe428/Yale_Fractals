Combinatorics in the Mandelbrot Set - Lavaurs Algorithm

Near the arcs we have written the period of the corresponding cycle, and so the period of the component of M formed when the arc is collapsed to a point.
For example, the 2 near the arc from 1/3 to 2/3 indicates this arc collapses to the point of tangency between the fixed point component (the big cardioid) and the large (2-cycle) disc.
The 3 near the arcs from 1/7 to 2/7 and from 5/7 to 6/7 indicate where the 3-cycle discs attach to the big cardioid, and so on for those arcs bounding the region containing the center of the unit circle.
If two components of M are tangent, the smaller period must divide the larger period.
So what about the period 4 arc connecting 3/15 and 4/15? The period division condition just mentioned prohibits this from collapsing to the point of tangency of a 3- and a 4-cycle component, so this period 4 arc must collapse to the root of a 4-cycle midget in the limb of M containing this 3-cycle component.
Similarly, the period 5 arcs connecting 5/31 to 6/31 and 7/31 to 8/31 collapse to the roots of 5-cycle midgets in the same limb.
