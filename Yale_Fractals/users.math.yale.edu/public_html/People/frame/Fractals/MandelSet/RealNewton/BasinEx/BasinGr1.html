Basin of Attraction Example

The function f(x) = x2 - 1 has two roots, x = 1 and x = -1. We find the basin of attraction of Newton's method for each root.

graph of the function
Take x0 > 1
Take x0 = 1
Take 1 > x0 > 0
Take x0 = 0
Take -1 < x0 < 0
Take x0 = -1
Take x0 < -1
First, starting with x0 > 1, we see Newton's method generates a sequence of points decreasing to the root x = 1. Certainly, the corresponding picture will be valid for any x0 > 1, so the basin of attraction of x = 1 includes all x0 > 1.

Consequently,

    the basin of attraction of x = 1 is all x0 > 0,
    and the basin of attraction of x = -1 is all x0 < 0.
